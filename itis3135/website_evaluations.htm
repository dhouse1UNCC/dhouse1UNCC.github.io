Website Evaluations

007 Museum

A (seemingly) fanmade website of all things James Bond, ranging from movies and video games to memorabilia. Has a timeline of the latest James Bond news, as well as information on the newest movie and the history of the franchise

  • Contrast: Not all information blocks are bordered, leaving some floating in whitespace. Urls don't look different from normal text, blue text is used with no consistency.
  • Repetition: No consistency in font sizes, font-alignment, or font colors. Many posts don't have a clear title or main focus.
  • Alignment: Alignment on the page is inconsistent, can see three similar elements all aligned differently on the same screen view. Alignment of text is also inconsistent.
  • Proximity: Posts are too close to each other and sometimes not seperated. Text and images are too close together and aligned in a abnormally








Craigslist

One of the best known online marketplaces, where you are able to post things for sale and search for things that others have posted. The equivilent of "for sale", "for hire" and "help wanted" newspaper ads on the internet.

  • Contrast: Special labels for post information, such as pricing and date of post. Easy to read font and good line spacing
  • Repetition: Consistent formating for every post, each post block in the catalog are uniform in size, text color, text size & font.
  • Alignment: All filters for posts on the left, dynamic site navigation at the top. All posts put in equal sized columns and rows.
  • Proximity: Perfect amount of spacing between sales posts. Everything feels coherent and navigatable.
